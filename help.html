How to use ProfitPilot

ProfitPilot is a decision tool: estimate profit & margin, reverse-calculate a required selling price, and run bulk checks fast — using your own inputs. The more accurate your assumptions, the better the output.

Step 1
Choose currency + optional FX
In the app, choose a display currency. If you buy in one currency and sell in another, you can enter an exchange rate manually (no live rates).
Tip: If you don’t need conversion, leave FX empty.
Step 2
Pick a preset or go manual
Presets are convenience examples. You can override values anytime. ProfitPilot estimates depend on the fee, returns, and ad assumptions you enter.
Important: Presets are not official marketplace fee tables.
Mode
Profit mode (default)
Enter:
  • Selling price
  • Product cost + shipping + packaging/misc
  • Fee %, fixed fee, ads %, return rate %
Then click Calculate.
What to watch: Profit and margin are the decision KPIs. “Fees/ads” shows the bleed.
Mode
Reverse mode (target profit → required price)
Use this when you know the profit you want per order. Enter a Target profit and ProfitPilot estimates the selling price needed to hit it.
Trial: Reverse mode may be limited during the trial to prevent abuse. PRO unlocks unlimited reverse calculations.
Mode
Bulk mode (table)
Bulk is for checking many product ideas quickly. You can:
  • Add rows and type values into the table
  • Paste values (Sell, Cost, Ship, Misc) and import
  • Apply current fees to all rows
  • Run bulk calculations (profit/margin per row)
  • Export bulk CSV
Best practice: Use Bulk to shortlist winners, then switch to Profit mode for deeper analysis.
Feature
Insights (PRO)
Insights provide decision signals to reduce bad listings:
  • Break-even price
  • Price needed to hit a target margin
  • Max ads % you can afford before loss
  • Risk hints based on margin sensitivity
Note: These are estimates from your inputs, not guarantees.
Feature
Export & sharing
Export helps you keep records or share a calculation:
  • CSV export (useful for analysis)
  • PDF export (print-friendly; PRO)
  • Share link (PRO)
Pro move: Save CSVs from Bulk mode and track your assumptions over time.
Reviews
Leave feedback (public)

Common mistakes
Why people get wrong profit numbers
Most errors come from missing costs or unrealistic assumptions:
  • Forgetting packaging, labels, inserts, or handling time
  • Not including returns/refunds rate (even small % matters)
  • Underestimating ad spend or promo discounts
  • Using the wrong fee % (category differences)
  • Ignoring shipping upgrades / surcharges
Reality check: A small fee or return change can flip a “winner” into a loss.
