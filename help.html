How to use ProfitPilot

This guide shows you exactly how to get accurate results (and avoid common mistakes).

Estimates only • Not advice

Before you start (important)

  • Use realistic numbers. Most “wrong results” come from unrealistic input assumptions.
  • If you’re unsure about a fee %, use a preset as a starting point, then adjust.
  • Always include hidden costs: packaging, labels, returns allowance, ad spend if you use it.

1) Profit Calculator (core)

Step-by-step
  • Choose a platform preset (or Manual).
  • Enter Selling price and Product cost.
  • Add Shipping + Packaging/Misc.
  • Adjust Platform fee % and Fixed fee if needed.
  • Click Calculate.
What to look at
  • Profit — money left after all costs & fees.
  • Margin — profit as % of selling price.
  • Fees + ads — your “platform tax”.
  • Return sensitivity — expected impact if returns happen.

2) Reverse Pricing (target profit → selling price)

Use this when you know what profit you want and you want the calculator to tell you the selling price needed.

  • Enter your product cost and other costs.
  • Set fee % and fixed fee (or use a preset).
  • Enter your target profit.
  • ProfitPilot outputs the minimum selling price required to hit that profit.

Tip: If the suggested price is too high vs market, the product may not be viable at your cost.

3) Competitor Comparison (market check)

  • Add competitor prices you see in the market.
  • ProfitPilot shows a safe range and highlights when your required price is above market.
  • This prevents “listing something that can’t profit” before you waste time.

4) Bulk Mode (table analysis)

  • Use bulk when you’re checking multiple products fast.
  • Fill rows with: price, cost, shipping, fees.
  • Export results to CSV to keep records and compare suppliers.

Export & Share

  • Export downloads your results (useful for bookkeeping or supplier comparisons).
  • Share creates a shareable summary (useful for partner decisions).

Trial vs PRO

  • Trial lets you test the tool (some advanced actions limited).
  • PRO is one-time unlock: unlimited reverse, competitor checks, bulk, and exports.

Common mistakes (and quick fixes)

  • Profit looks too high: you forgot fees, ads, or packaging.
  • Profit looks negative: your selling price is too low OR fees are too high OR cost is too high.
  • Numbers seem weird: ensure you didn’t type commas incorrectly (e.g. 1,000.50 is ok).
